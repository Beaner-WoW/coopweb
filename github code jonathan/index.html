TOP PACK PLACES

STATUE OF LIBERTY

The Statue of Liberty is famous because it's a powerful, universal symbol of freedom, democracy, and opportunity, given by France to the U.S. as a gesture of friendship and to commemorate American independence.

  • SYMBOL OF LIBERTY AND DEMOCRACY
  • ICONIC DESIGN
  • GATEWAY TO AMERICA
    • CENTRAL PARK

    Central Park is famous for being America's first major landscaped public park, a revolutionary urban green space designed by Olmsted and Vaux to offer nature's respite within bustling Manhattan, becoming a cultural icon

  • SYMBOL OF NYC
  • POP CULTURE
  • PIONEERING PUBLIC SPACE
    • TIME SQUARE

    Times Square is immensely popular due to its status as the "Crossroads of the World," offering a sensory-overload experience with massive, iconic digital billboards, 24/7 energy, and the famous annual New Year's Eve ball drop.

  • ICONIC ATMOSPHERE AND VISUALS
  • ENTERAINMENT AND CULTURE
  • SHOPPING AND DINING

    Roosevelt island tramway

    The Roosevelt Island Tramway, opening in 1976 as North America's first commuter aerial tram, operates 250 feet above the East River, offering skyline views for a standard MTA subway fare.
