THE MET

The Metropolitan Museum of Art ("The Met") in NYC is one of the world's largest, most comprehensive art museums, featuring over 2 million works spanning 5,000 years. Located on Museum Mile, it is special for its unparalleled, encyclopedic collection—ranging from Egyptian temples to European painting—paired with its massive, iconic Beaux-Arts building on Central Park.

THE AMERICAN MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY

The American Museum of Natural History (AMNH) in NYC is world-renowned for its massive collection of over 33 million specimens, spanning 4.5 billion years of history across 45+ permanent halls. Key highlights include the iconic dinosaur wing, the 94-foot Blue Whale model, the Hayden Planetarium, and the immersive Gilder Center, making it a premier, all-encompassing, and interactive scientific institution.

THE MUSEUM OF MODERN ART

he Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in NYC is world-renowned for its unrivaled, comprehensive collection of 20th and 21st-century art, featuring iconic masterpieces like Van Gog'hs Starry Night and Picasso's Les Demoiselles d'Avignon. As the first institution devoted exclusively to modern art, it shapes global art, spanning painting, film, design, and performance.

Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum

The Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum is iconic for its revolutionary Frank Lloyd Wright-designed building, featuring a unique, continuous spiral ramp that redefines art viewing. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it offers an immersive, non-traditional experience, focusing on modern and contemporary art in an open, organic, and highly,, distinct,, architectural setting.

9/11 memorial and museum

The 9/11 Memorial & Museum is the country's principal institution to honor the memories of the victims, preserve the history, and explore the complex impact of both 9/11 and the World Trade Center bombing of February 26, 1993.
